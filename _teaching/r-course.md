Concerning theoretical statistics, this course teaches the same quantitative methods as my SPSS course. However, I had the opportunity to design this course from scratch myself, which was not the case with SPSS. I wanted it to be much more hands-on. I try to use as much of modern R as possible, since, while base R is a great thing, it is nothing that can be taught easily in one single social science statistics course. Modern R with its many convenient packages is also much less frustrating for students. When it comes to using R, in this course students learn

    * how to use the tidyverse,
    * various tidyverse functions for data manipulation (filter, select, case_when, etc.),
    * how to plot data using ggplot2,
    * doing cross-tabulation with the package gmodels,
    * to use the package modendive for regression analysis,
    * to do a T-test or ANOVA in R.

During the Covid-19 pandemic, I recorded short videos for my students that diverge from the common 90 minutes course format. Pretty much everyone preferred video lengths of five to 20 minutes over a longer video, so I divided each session into smaller topics. I bought a whiteboard to explain the theoretical part and then recorded my screen to show how analyses are conducted. From April 2020 to April 2021 I tought remotely from home.
