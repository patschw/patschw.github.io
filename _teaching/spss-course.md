My first full-fledged course, which I was allowed to teach myself, was an introduction to the statistical software SPSS. People coming to my course are usually undergraduates and already know the basics of quantitative methods but have had little statistics. When finished students can do basic data management tasks on datasets, like

* filtering variables,
* selecting variables,
* recoding variables,
* and computing new variables.

After the course, students can furthermore apply and interpret methods of descriptive and inferential statistics like

* measures of central tendency,
* measures of spread,
* cross-tabulation,
* tests for differences in mean (e.g. T-Test),
* tests for statistical significance,
* correlation,
* linear Regression,
* and Analysis of Variance.

In this course I teach them more advanced statistical theory like linear regression and ANOVA and also, of course, they learn how to conduct such analysis.
